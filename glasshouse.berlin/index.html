Glasshouse is a space experimenting with Machine Learning and IoT to grow plants indoors. We are interested in how technology can help people keep more houseplants in their urban environment by removing previous obsticals. Why plants? because beyond increasing the levels of oxygen indoors, removing toxins from the air and lowering stress levels we think plants make us better humans.

Research

NASA did an extensive study on the effects of certain common indoor plants ability to provide a natural way of removing toxic agents such as benzene, formaldehyde and trichloroethylene from the air, helping neutralize the effects of Volatile organic compounds (VOCs)emitted as gases from certain household items. NASA researchers suggest efficient air cleaning is accomplished with at least one plant per 100 square feet of home or office space.

  • Benzene
  • Ammonia
  • Formaldehyde
  • Trichloroethylene
  • Xylene
  • Toluene

Science confirms how important plants are to our daily lives, confirming that plants are the best solution to improving indoor air quality. Yet it is a continual task to keep plants alive in these environments, thats why we designed -

Glasshouse Cube

Glasshouse Cube is a prototype for a self contained plant system. The cube not only keeps the plant alive without any human assistance but enhances its natural abilities to improve air quality and remove toxins. Monitoring the plants output using IoT sensors and image recognition to keep it in an optimum environment.
